## Clinical Reasoning Framework

> [!quote] Dr. Wang Jūyì 王居易
> *"Diagnosis is a very rigorous mental exercise. The practitioner is like a sleuth looking to connect all of the signs/symptoms to one of the six levels."*



### Symptom-Pattern-Structure 症候結構

The diagnostic process integrates four methods around the primary symptom:

```
              Tongue
                │
    Palpation ──┼── Asking
                │
              Pulse
         ［Primary Symptom］
```

This framework is called **症候結構** *zhèng hòu jié gòu* — the primary complaint sits at the center, with all diagnostic findings converging to illuminate the pattern.



### Interpreting Palpation

> [!info] Five Questions for Palpation Findings
> 1. **What channel?**
> 2. **Point zone?** — Is the finding at a specific point area?
> 3. **Point category?** — Source, cleft, sea, etc.?
> 4. **Clinical use?** — How is this point applied?
> 5. **Quality?** — Texture, depth, character of finding?



#### Docking 對接 *duì jiē*

Palpated changes on channels *unrelated* to the chief complaint help differentiate which channel system is ultimately involved. These findings must be "docked" — integrated into the overall clinical picture.

## Channel Differentiation

**辨經** *biàn jīng* — the first major step in clinical reasoning.

```
Chief Complaint  →  Symptom-Pattern-Structure  →  Channel Differentiation
                              │
                    Understanding of Qì Dynamic
```



> [!important] Three Qì Concepts
> | Term | Chinese | Pinyin | Meaning |
> |------|---------|--------|---------|
> | Qì Dynamic | 氣機 | *qì jī* | Mechanism/movement of qì |
> | Qì Transformation | 氣化 | *qì huà* | Transformative processes |
> | Function | 功能 | *gōng néng* | Functional activity |

## Six Channel Framework

### Thinking in Six Instead of Twelve

Rather than twelve separate channels, the six-channel model pairs yīn and yáng channels sharing similar qì dynamics:



| Yīn Channel | Organs | | Yáng Channel | Organs |
|:-----------:|:------:|:---:|:------------:|:------:|
| **Tài Yīn** 太陰 | [[Lung]] / [[Spleen]] | ↔ | **Yáng Míng** 陽明 | [[Large Intestine]] / [[Stomach]] |
| **Shào Yīn** 少陰 | [[Heart]] / [[Kidney]] | ↔ | **Tài Yáng** 太陽 | [[Small Intestine]] / [[Bladder]] |
| **Jué Yīn** 厥陰 | [[Pericardium]] / [[Liver]] | ↔ | **Shào Yáng** 少陽 | [[Triple Burner]] / [[Gallbladder]] |



### Channel Physiology Overview

| Qì | Chinese | Channel | Organs | Movement |
|:--:|:-------:|:-------:|:------:|:--------:|
| Cold | 寒 *hán* | **Tài Yáng** | SI + UB | **Open** |
| Fire | 火 *huǒ* | **Shào Yáng** | SJ + GB | **Pivot** |
| Dryness | 燥 *zào* | **Yáng Míng** | LI + ST | **Close** |
| Dampness | 濕 *shī* | **Tài Yīn** | LU + SP | **Open** |
| Summerheat | 暑 *shǔ* | **Shào Yīn** | HT + KI | **Pivot** |
| Wind | 風 *fēng* | **Jué Yīn** | LV + PC | **Close** |



### Open-Pivot-Close Dynamics

> [!example] Method Six: Similar Movements Synergize
> 
> **Open 開** — Tài Yáng & Tài Yīn
> Opens up and outward; releases the exterior and "exterior of the interior"
> 
> **Pivot 樞** — Shào Yáng & Shào Yīn
> Regulates heat; relates to [[Source Qi|source qì]] and [[Ministerial Fire|ministerial fire]]
> 
> **Close 闔** — Yáng Míng & Jué Yīn
> Themes of movement with tendencies toward stasis

## Channel Selection Strategy

**選經** *xuǎn jīng* — follows channel differentiation.

```
Differentiated Channel  →  Desired Qì Dynamic Effect  →  Channel Selection
```



### Internal/External Strategies

> [!note] Treatment Channel Selection
> | Differentiation | Pattern | Treatment Strategy |
> |-----------------|---------|-------------------|
> | Yīn channel primary | Deficiency | Treat yīn channel directly |
> | Yīn channel primary | Excess | Use paired yáng channel |
> | Yáng channel primary | Deficiency | Use paired yīn channel |
> | Yáng channel primary | Excess | Treat yáng channel directly |



**For Spleen (Tài Yīn):**
- Spleen deficiency → Treat Spleen channel directly
- Spleen excess (damp-heat) → Consider Stomach channel (Yáng Míng pair)

## Foot Points

### SP-1 隱白 *Yǐn Bái* — Hidden White

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | [[Well Point|Jǐng-Well]] (Wood) |
| **Location** | Medial corner of great toe, 0.1 cun from nail |

**Primary Use:** Spotting (mid-cycle bleeding) with direct moxa.

> [!tip] Spotting 漏 vs. Flooding 崩
> - **漏 *lòu*** (spotting) — Spleen not controlling [[Blood|blood]]  → **SP-1**
> - **崩 *bēng*** (flooding) — Liver not holding blood → different approach



---

### SP-3 太白 *Tài Bái* — Supreme White

|              |                                                             |                                    |                      |
| ------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------- | -------------------- |
| **Type**     | [[Source Point                                              | Yuán-Source]] · [[Shu-Stream Point | Shū-Stream]] (Earth) |
| **Location** | Proximal to head of 1st metatarsal, red-white skin junction |                                    |                      |
|              |                                                             |                                    |                      |

**Primary Use:** All manifestations of [[Spleen Qi Deficiency|Spleen qì deficiency]].

> [!tip] Daytime Urination
> SP-3 for **frequent daytime urination** — combine with [[LU-9]] to strengthen the Tài Yīn axis.



---

### SP-4 公孫 *Gōng Sūn* — Grandfather-Grandson

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | [[Luo-Connecting Point|Luò-Connecting]] |
| **Type** | [[Confluent Point]] of [[Chong Mai|Chōng Mài]] 衝脈 |
| **Location** | Anterior-inferior to base of 1st metatarsal |

> [!important] Two Key Functions
> 1. **Collateral function** — "Upstream" microcirculation issues in the channel
> 2. **Confluent point** (八脈交會穴) — Opens the Chōng Mài, "Sea of Blood"

**Primary Use:** Gynecological blood disorders, digestive issues, Chōng vessel patterns.

---



## Lower Leg Points

### SP-6 三陰交 *Sān Yīn Jiāo* — Three Yīn Meeting

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | [[Meeting Point]] of 3 leg yīn channels |
| **Channels** | [[Spleen]] · [[Liver]] · [[Kidney]] |
| **Location** | 3 cun above medial malleolus, posterior to tibia |

**Primary Use:** Strong affinity for **lower jiāo** — essential for gynecological and urogenital conditions.

> [!tip] Blood Deficiency Protocol
> **SP-6 + [[REN-4]]** with moxa — classic for [[Blood Deficiency|blood deficiency]]



---

### SP-8 地機 *Dì Jī* — Earth Mechanism

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | [[Cleft Point|Xī-Cleft]] |
| **Location** | 3 cun below SP-9 |

> [!danger] Key Indication: Painful Menstruation
> The famous point for **痛經** *tòng jīng* (dysmenorrhea), especially with [[Blood Stasis|blood stasis]].

**Primary Use:** Unblocks Tài Yīn; blood stasis gynecology.



---

### SP-9 陰陵泉 *Yīn Líng Quán* — Yīn Mound Spring

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | [[Sea Point|Hé-Sea]] (Water) |
| **Location** | Depression below medial condyle of tibia |

**Primary Use:** Tài Yīn [[Dampness|damp]] patterns.

> [!tip] Tài Yīn Counterflow
> **SP-9 + [[LU-5]]** — addresses lung-spleen axis when qì rebels upward.

## Thigh and Torso Points

### SP-10 血海 *Xuè Hǎi* — Sea of Blood

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Regular point |
| **Location** | 2 cun above mediosuperior border of patella |

**Primary Use:** **Invigorates and cools blood** — dual action for versatility.

> [!example] Common Combinations
> - **SP-10 + SP-4** or **SP-8** → Gynecological blood patterns
> - **SP-10** alone → Dermatology (cools blood for skin heat)



---

### SP-14 腹結 *Fù Jié* — Abdominal Knot

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Regular point |
| **Location** | 4 cun lateral to midline, 1.3 cun below umbilicus |

**Primary Use:** Constipation.

> [!danger] McBurney's Point Correlation
> Right SP-14 ≈ **McBurney's Point** (⅓ ASIS to umbilicus)
> 
> **Rebound pain here = rule out appendicitis!**



---

### SP-15 大橫 *Dà Héng* — Great Horizontal

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Regular point |
| **Location** | 4 cun lateral to umbilicus (level with [[ST-25]]) |

**Primary Use:** **Cold-type diarrhea** — cold excess or yáng deficiency. Use moxa.

> [!note] Compare with ST-25
> Same level, different indications:
> - **SP-15** → Diarrhea (cold)
> - **ST-25** 天樞 → Constipation



---

### SP-21 大包 *Dà Bāo* — Great Embracement

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | [[Great Collateral|Great Collateral of the Spleen]] 脾大絡 |
| **Location** | Mid-axillary line, 6th intercostal space |

> [!important] Unique Anatomy
> SP-21 connects to the **脾大絡** *pí dà luò* — an extra collateral beyond the standard fifteen!

**Indications:**
- Pain throughout the body (the great collateral "wraps" everything)
- Palpable nodule here may indicate chronic lung pathology

## Location Study Guide

> [!info] Study Method
> Cover the **How to Locate** column. Use landmarks and cun to find each point, then check.

### Foot — SP-1 to SP-5

| Point | Name | Cun | How to Locate |
|:-----:|:----:|:---:|---------------|
| **SP-1** | 隱白 | 0.1 | Junction of medial and proximal borders of great toe nail |
| **SP-2** | 大都 | — | Medial great toe, distal-inferior to 1st MTP joint, red-white skin |
| **SP-3** | 太白 | — | Depression proximal-inferior to head of 1st metatarsal, red-white skin |
| **SP-4** | 公孫 | — | Depression distal-inferior to base of 1st metatarsal, red-white skin |
| **SP-5** | 商丘 | — | Distal-inferior to medial malleolus, midway between navicular tuberosity and malleolar tip |

> [!tip] Red-White Skin Junction
> SP-2, SP-3, SP-4 all follow this line on the medial foot — palpate from toe toward ankle.



---

### Lower Leg — SP-6 to SP-9

| Point | Name | Cun | How to Locate |
|:-----:|:----:|:---:|---------------|
| **SP-6** | 三陰交 | 3 | 3 cun above medial malleolus, posterior border of tibia |
| **SP-7** | 漏谷 | 6 | 6 cun above medial malleolus, posterior to medial tibial border |
| **SP-8** | 地機 | 3 below SP-9 | Line from SP-9 to malleolus, 3 cun below SP-9 |
| **SP-9** | 陰陵泉 | — | Depression below medial tibial condyle, between tibia and gastrocnemius |

> [!note] Proportional Measure
> Medial malleolus → medial knee crease = **13 cun**
> - SP-6 = 3 cun up
> - SP-7 = 6 cun up  
> - SP-8 = 10 cun up (or 3 below SP-9)
> - SP-9 = at the condyle

> [!tip] Finding SP-9
> Slide finger up posterior tibial border from malleolus — SP-9 is where you fall into the depression below the condyle.



---

### Thigh — SP-10 to SP-12

| Point | Name | Cun | How to Locate |
|:-----:|:----:|:---:|---------------|
| **SP-10** | 血海 | 2 | Knee flexed; 2 cun above mediosuperior patella, on vastus medialis |
| **SP-11** | 箕門 | 6 above SP-10 | On line connecting SP-10 and SP-12 |
| **SP-12** | 衝門 | 3.5 lateral | Lateral end of inguinal groove, lateral to femoral artery |

> [!tip] Quick SP-10 Method
> Cup opposite knee (right hand → left knee). SP-10 is where your thumb naturally rests.

> [!warning] SP-12: Femoral Artery
> Always palpate for pulse — needle **lateral** to artery.



---

### Abdomen — SP-13 to SP-16

| Point | Name | Lateral | Vertical | How to Locate |
|:-----:|:----:|:-------:|:--------:|---------------|
| **SP-13** | 府舍 | 4 cun | 0.7 above SP-12 | 4 cun lateral to midline, level with REN-2 |
| **SP-14** | 腹結 | 4 cun | 1.3 below umbilicus | 4 cun lateral, 1.3 cun below umbilicus level |
| **SP-15** | 大橫 | 4 cun | Umbilicus level | 4 cun lateral to center of umbilicus |
| **SP-16** | 腹哀 | 4 cun | 3 above umbilicus | 4 cun lateral, 3 cun above umbilicus |

> [!note] The 4-Cun Lateral Line
> SP-13 through SP-16 all lie **4 cun lateral to midline** — just at or beyond the lateral rectus border.



---

### Chest — SP-17 to SP-20

| Point | Name | Lateral | ICS | How to Locate |
|:-----:|:----:|:-------:|:---:|---------------|
| **SP-17** | 食竇 | 6 cun | 5th | 6 cun lateral to anterior midline, 5th intercostal space |
| **SP-18** | 天谿 | 6 cun | 4th | 6 cun lateral, 4th intercostal space |
| **SP-19** | 胸鄉 | 6 cun | 3rd | 6 cun lateral, 3rd intercostal space |
| **SP-20** | 周榮 | 6 cun | 2nd | 6 cun lateral, 2nd intercostal space |

> [!note] The 6-Cun Lateral Line
> SP-17 through SP-20 ascend on a line **6 cun lateral** to midline.
> ICS pattern: **5 → 4 → 3 → 2** (ascending)

> [!tip] Finding Intercostal Spaces
> - Sternal angle = level with **2nd rib**
> - Nipple (men) = typically **4th ICS**
> - Count from either landmark
