# The Big Picture: Six Stages at a Glance {#the-big-picture}

## What Are We Really Asking?

When you see a patient, you're answering three fundamental questions:

1. **Where is the pathogen?** (Stage/Location)
2. **What is it doing there?** (Pathomechanism)
3. **What does the body need?** (Treatment principle)

&nbsp;

## The Six Stages Overview

| Stage | Location | Temperature | Core Dynamic | Treatment Principle |
|-------|----------|-------------|--------------|---------------------|
| **太陽 Tàiyáng** | Exterior (surface) | Fever + Chills together | Pathogen at gate, Wei/Ying disharmony | Release exterior |
| **陽明 Yángmíng** | Interior (bowels) | High fever, NO chills | Excess heat, formed or formless | Clear heat, purge if formed |
| **少陽 Shàoyáng** | Half-exterior/half-interior | Alternating fever/chills | Qi dynamic disruption, ministerial fire blocked | Harmonize |
| **太陰 Tàiyīn** | Middle burner (Spleen) | NO fever, mild cold | Deficiency cold, dampness | Warm middle, tonify |
| **少陰 Shàoyīn** | Deepest (Heart/Kidney) | NO fever, severe cold OR insomnia | Yang collapse OR Yin deficiency | Rescue yang OR nourish yin |
| **厥陰 Juéyīn** | Liver, Pericardium | Variable, hot-cold mixed | Qi constraint, blood deficiency, or wood-earth | Disperse, nourish blood, harmonize |

&nbsp;

## The Critical Insight

> **Depth ≠ Severity**
> 
> A Shàoyīn patient is not necessarily sicker than a Tàiyáng patient. But the *stakes* are higher—Heart and Kidney are fundamental to life itself. Most lines about death in the Shānghán Lùn appear in the Shàoyīn section.

# Systems Comparison Tables {#systems-comparison}

## How to Use These Tables

Look at ONE symptom → scan across ALL stages → identify which pattern fits your patient.

The power is in the comparison, not memorizing each stage in isolation.

## Clinical Assessment Questions

Ask yourself:

1. **"Does the patient like warmth or avoid it?"**
   - Likes warmth → Yang deficiency (Sì Nì Tāng)
   - Avoids warmth → Yang constraint (Sì Nì Sǎn)
   - Neutral → Blood deficiency (Dāng Guī Sì Nì Tāng)

2. **"How is their energy?"**
   - Fatigued, wants to sleep → Yang deficiency
   - Variable, may be irritable → Yang constraint
   - Active, energetic → Blood deficiency

3. **"How far does the cold extend?"**
   - To elbows/knees → Yang deficiency
   - Just fingers/toes → Constraint or blood deficiency

# Sweating Patterns {#sweating-patterns}

## Sweating as Diagnostic Marker

| Sweating Pattern | What It Means | Formula Direction |
|-----------------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Spontaneous, non-productive** (doesn't resolve illness) | Wei/Ying disharmony, pores too open | Guì Zhī Tāng (harmonize, not open more) |
| **No sweating** with exterior signs | Pores locked tight | Má Huáng Tāng (open pores) |
| **Profuse, full body** with high fever | Yang Ming channel heat | Bái Hǔ Tāng (clear heat) |
| **Limited** (head/extremities only) with constipation | Yang Ming organ, heat bound | Dà Chéng Qì Tāng (purge) |
| **Night sweats** with yin deficiency signs | Yin failing to contain yang at night | Nourish yin |

**The Cost of Sweating:** Every time you sweat, you lose yang qi AND yin fluids. This is why incorrect sweating treatment can damage the patient.

# Hot-Cold Complex Patterns {#hot-cold-complex}

## When It's Not Simply Hot OR Cold

Two main patterns present with heat above and cold below:

### Bàn Xià Xiè Xīn Tāng vs. Wú Méi Wán

| Feature | Bàn Xià Xiè Xīn Tāng | Wú Méi Wán |
|---------|---------------------|------------|
| **Origin** | Transmuted Tàiyáng (mistreatment) | Juéyīn Wood-Earth |
| **Mechanism** | Spleen/Stomach reversal | Liver overacting on Spleen |
| **Emotional connection?** | NO - "just always been this way" | YES - emotions trigger symptoms |
| **Trigger pattern** | No clear cause | Anger, frustration → GI flare |
| **Heat symptoms** | Reflux, belching, mouth sores | Similar heat rising |
| **Cold symptoms** | Borborygmus, diarrhea | Similar cold below |
| **Key question** | "Has this been lifelong?" | "Does stress trigger your gut?" |

**Clinical Pearl:** If the patient says "When I get angry/stressed, my stomach acts up" → think Wú Méi Wán. If they say "I've just always had a sensitive stomach, no idea why" → think Bàn Xià Xiè Xīn Tāng.

| Formula | Pattern | Key Indications | Don't Confuse With |

# Transmuted Patterns {#transmuted-patterns}

## What Are Transmuted Patterns?

When a Tàiyáng pathogen doesn't resolve normally, it can "transmute" into interior patterns that are no longer channel-based. These often result from mistreatment (wrong sweating, purging, etc.).

## Key Transmuted Patterns

### Water vs. Blood Amassment

| Feature | Wǔ Líng Sǎn (Water) | Táo Hé Chéng Qì Tāng (Blood) |
|---------|---------------------|------------------------------|
| **What's stuck** | Fluids (Water Qi) | Blood (Stasis) |
| **Urination** | INHIBITED | NORMAL |
| **Thirst** | Unquenchable, vomits fluids | Not prominent |
| **Mental state** | — | Mania, vexation |
| **Lower abdomen** | — | Tense, bound |
| **Mechanism** | Bladder Qi transformation impaired | Stasis Heat (Yù Huǒ) harassing Heart |

**The Question:** Is it a water problem or a blood problem?
- Water: urination affected, thirst prominent
- Blood: urination normal, mental symptoms prominent

### Chest Patterns

| Feature | Zhī Zǐ Chǐ Tāng | Xiǎo Xiàn Xiōng Tāng |
|---------|-----------------|---------------------|

# Key Differentiations Summary {#key-differentiations}

## The "Don't Confuse These" List

### 1. Three Sì Nì Formulas
- **Sì Nì Tāng** = Yang deficiency, cold core, tonify yang
- **Sì Nì Sǎn** = Yang constraint, hot core, disperse
- **Dāng Guī Sì Nì Tāng** = Blood deficiency, normal core, build blood

### 2. Digested vs. Undigested Food in Diarrhea
- **Digested** = Tàiyīn (Spleen yang weak but still some function)
- **Undigested** = Shàoyīn (Kidney yang failing, more severe)

### 3. Thirst Relieved vs. Not Relieved
- **Relieved by drinking** = True heat consuming fluids
- **Not relieved** = Fluid circulation problem, not fluid deficiency

### 4. Fever + Chills Pattern
- **Simultaneous** = Tàiyáng (pathogen at surface)
- **Alternating** = Shàoyáng (pathogen in between)
- **Fever, no chills** = Yángmíng (full interior heat)

### 5. Sweating in Tàiyáng
- **Present** = Wind Strike → Guì Zhī Tāng
- **Absent** = Cold Damage → Má Huáng Tāng

### 6. Hot-Cold Mixed with GI
- **Emotion-triggered** = Juéyīn Wood-Earth → Wú Méi Wán
- **No clear trigger** = Transmuted → Bàn Xià Xiè Xīn Tāng

### 7. Heat in Chest Patterns
- **Yin deficiency signs present** = Shàoyīn Heat → Huáng Lián È Jiāo Tāng
- **No deficiency signs** = Transmuted → Zhī Zǐ Chǐ Tāng

### 8. Water vs. Blood in Lower Burner
- **Urination inhibited** = Water Amassment → Wǔ Líng Sǎn
- **Urination normal, mental symptoms** = Blood Amassment → Táo Hé Chéng Qì Tāng

# Final Review: The Essential Questions

When you see any patient, run through these:

1. **Is there still an exterior pattern?** (Fever + chills together, floating pulse)
   - Yes → Don't purge, don't tonify heavily (except Shàoyáng)

2. **Where is the heat/cold?**
   - Full body vs. localized
   - Core vs. extremities
   - Upper vs. lower

3. **What's the fluid status?**
   - Thirsty or not?
   - Sweating or not?
   - Urination normal?
   - Diarrhea?

4. **What's the energy/spirit?**
   - Fatigued → likely deficiency
   - Irritable/manic → likely heat or constraint
   - Wants to sleep → Shàoyīn or severe deficiency

5. **What makes it better or worse?**
   - Better with warmth/pressure → deficiency cold
   - Worse with pressure → excess
   - Worse with heat → true heat pattern
